Creating Own Path

There was a girl who dreamed of becoming k-pop star while growing up in South Korea. She went to countless auditions, singing in front of people to show her potential-- but she received countless rejections. She quickly realized that what she dreamed was merely a fantasy.



She loved philosophy, and Plato's book "Republic" was completely eye-opening for her. She was hooked by the beautiful conversations of Socrates and other philosophers. She realized the power of logical thinking and questioned why people do things the way they do.



She also loved learning languages, and always wanted to study English. Her best friend invited her to stay with her in Houston, Texas and attend an English language school. Thus, her new journey began.



After she felt comfortable speaking in English, she wanted to experience studying in college in the USA. She chose to study Philosophy and Religious Studies at Pace University and continued on for her Master's degree in Philosophy and Education at Columbia University.



During her studies at Columbia University, she started dreaming of becoming an educator. She thought of her teachers in middle school and a high school. She remembered the math teacher who made her doubt her math skills. Then, she decided to become a math teacher. Why? She was confident. Not just in math-- but also confident in teaching math for those who struggle just like her in her childhood.



She enjoyed teaching students in middle school and high school for two years. She started asking questions about how to improve the curriculum. Could there be a better grading system that saves teachers' time? Could there be software tools that reduce administrative paperwork? How amazing would it be to create those tools?



She paused, and began to explore the world of creation-- specifically in softwre development. She wanted to be part of the world with people who create anything from zero.
