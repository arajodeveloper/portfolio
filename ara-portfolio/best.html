Creating Own Path

There was a girl who dreamed to be a k-pop start growing up in South Korea. She went countless auditions signing in front of people to see her potential-- but what she only received was also countless rejections. She quickly realizes that what she dreamed was merely a fantasy. She takes on her different path in the academic world.



She loved Philosophy. A book, "The Republic" by Plato was a completely eye-opening for her. She was hooked by beautiful conversation by Socrates and realized the power of logical thinking in life and countless questions why people do certain things on what people do.



She also loved learaning languages. She has always wanted to study English language. Her best friend invited her to stay with her in Houston, Texas and attended the English langague school. Then, her new journey started.



When she felt comfortable speaking in English, she wanted to see what it would be like studying in college in the USA. She chose a Philosophy and Religious Studies major to study in Pace University and suprisingly, she decides to pursue Master's degree in Philosophy and Education in Columbia University.



During her study in Columbia University, she started dreaming of becoming an educator. She starts thinking of her teachers in middle school and a high school. She remembers the worst math teacher who helped her nothing but to hate math for the rest of her school life. Then, she decides to become a math teacher. Why? She was confident. Not just in math-- but also confident in teaching math for those who struggle just like her in her childhood.



She enjoyed teaching students in a middle school and a high school for two years. She started asking questions about what existed in current education. Can there be better grading system for teachers to save time? Can there be a software that could store all the datas so that we are not flying around with pieces of paper? How amazing would it be the person who creates something from zero? She thought.



She paused, and began to explore the world of creation-- speicifically in softwre development. She wanted to be part of the world with people who create anything from zero.
